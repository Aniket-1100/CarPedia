Nissan Skyline Fifth Generation

❮ ❯

The Nissan Skyline GT-R R34 is a legendary high-performance sports car that was produced by Nissan from 1999 to 2002. The R34 GT-R was powered by Nissan's legendary RB26DETT engine, a twin-turbocharged 2.6-liter inline-six that produced 280 horsepower (limited due to a Japanese gentlemen's agreement) and 276 lb-ft of torque in stock form. However, tuners have been able to extract well over 500 horsepower from this engine. The R34 GT-R was also equipped with Nissan's ATTESA E-TS all-wheel-drive system, which provided superior handling and grip.

1. Engine Options:

The Nissan Skyline GT-R R34 actually only came with one engine option: the legendary RB26DETT. It's a 2.6-liter inline-six engine with a twin-turbocharger setup. While official figures state 280 horsepower (due to a Japanese gentlemen's agreement limiting power output), many believe the actual output was closer to 330 horsepower.

2. Performance:

The R34 GT-R produces around 280 horsepower in its stock form, though it is known for being easily tunable for significantly higher power outputs. It features Nissan's advanced ATTESA E-TS all-wheel-drive system providing excellent handling characteristics. The R34 is known for its sharp handling and stability, thanks to its well-balanced chassis, advanced suspension (variations depending on trim), and all-wheel-drive system.

3. Body Design:

The Nissan Skyline GT-R R34's body design is a masterpiece of form following function, creating a timeless and aerodynamic silhouette. he R34's overall shape is smooth and curved, with a low profile that minimizes drag and optimizes airflow. The signature circular headlights are instantly recognizable and add a touch of aggression to the front fascia. The R34's taillights are round and housed within a unique quad-circle design, another iconic feature.

4. Wheel Design:

The wheels often featured a split-spoke design, which not only looked sporty but also helped with brake cooling and reducing overall weight. To enhance performance, the wheels were typically made from lightweight alloy materials, such as aluminum, to reduce unsprung weight and improve handling. The GT-R R34 came with wheels of substantial diameter to accommodate large brake rotors and calipers, necessary for high-performance braking.

5. Collectibility:

The R34 GT-R is widely recognized as an iconic and legendary sports car, particularly due to its performance pedigree and association with motorsport success. The car gained significant cultural significance through its appearances in movies, video games, and popular media, which has contributed to its enduring popularity and collectible status. Overall, the Nissan GT-R R34 represents a pinnacle of Japanese sports car engineering and has attained an iconic status in automotive history, making it a highly sought-after collectible that continues to appreciate in value.

6. Racing Heritage

While the R34 itself might not have as many official race wins, it became a favorite among tuners and privateer racing teams due to its advanced all-wheel-drive system and powerful engine, allowing it to compete and win in various racing series. The R34's association with the popular culture phenomenon "Fast and the Furious" franchise further solidified its image as a high-performance, racing-inspired machine.

7. Technological Features

The Nissan Skyline GT-R R34 boasted some impressive technological features that made it a formidable force on the road. This advanced all-wheel-drive system (Advanced Total Traction Engineering System for All-Terrain with Electronic Torque Split) is a highlight. It constantly monitors driving conditions and distributes torque between the front and rear wheels for optimal grip and stability. A 16-bit computer analyzes sensor data 10 times per second to achieve this feat. This driver-centric system displayed a range of real-time performance data like boost pressure, oil pressure, and lap times.

8. Upgraded Transmission:

Replacing stock clutch plates, friction materials, and other internal parts with stronger, higher-performance versions allows the transmission to handle more torque and power. The valve body controls how fluid flows within the transmission, affecting shift speed and firmness. Upgraded valve bodies can provide quicker, firmer shifts and better control over gear selection, especially beneficial for performance driving.

9. Advanced Traction Control System:

The GT-R R34 utilizes Nissan's remarkable ATTESA E-TS AWD (Advanced Total Traction Engineering System for All-Terrain with Electronic Torque Split). This system continuously monitors various factors like wheel speed, steering angle, and engine output. Using a 16-bit computer that analyzes sensor data 10 times per second, ATTESA E-TS intelligently distributes torque between the front and rear wheels for optimal traction and stability in all driving conditions.

10. Safety Features:

The Nissan Skyline GT-R R34, while a performance beast, wasn't exactly packed with the advanced safety features we see in modern cars.
  • Three-point seatbelts: Standard equipment, crucial for occupant safety in a crash.
  • Anti-lock Braking System (ABS): This helped prevent wheel lockup during hard braking, allowing the driver to maintain steering control.
  • Central locking: Though not directly related to safety during operation, it did offer a basic level of security against unauthorized entry.
