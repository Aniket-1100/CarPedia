The time period of the second generation of the Nissan Skyline GT-R, known as the "Kenmeri" generation, spans from the early 1970s to the mid-1970s. This period was marked by significant social, economic, and technological changes globally, which influenced the automotive industry and the fate of the GT-R lineage. The oil crisis had a profound impact on the automotive industry, as consumers shifted towards smaller, more fuel-efficient vehicles. This prompted automakers to reconsider their product offerings and prioritize fuel economy and emissions compliance. Skyline second generation was marked by economic turbulence, environmental concerns, and shifts in consumer preferences, all of which influenced the automotive industry and the fate of high-performance models like the GT-R. While the GT-R did not see production during this period, its legacy endured, leading to its eventual resurgence in the late 1980s with the R32 generation.

1. Engine Options:

Inline-six cylinder engine, various displacements including 2.0 liters and 2.4 liters. Power outputs varied depending on the specific engine configuration, ranging from approximately 120 to 160 horsepower. Available with either a 4-speed manual or 3-speed automatic transmission. Acceleration from 0 to 60 mph (0 to 97 km/h) in around 9-11 seconds, with top speeds ranging from 110 to 125 mph (177 to 201 km/h). The Kenmeri Skyline featured a comfortable and spacious interior, advanced for its time, with options such as air conditioning, power windows, and AM/FM radio.

2. Trim Levels:

During the second generation of the Nissan Skyline, which includes the "Kenmeri" era, the Skyline lineup consisted of various trim levels, including different engine options:
  • Nissan Skyline 2000GT: This was the base model of the second-generation Skyline lineup. It typically featured a naturally aspirated inline-six engine, providing adequate performance for daily driving. It offered basic features and options suitable for a comfortable commuting experience.

  • Nissan Skyline 2000GT-X: The GT-X trim level represented a sportier variant of the Skyline lineup. It often came equipped with performance-enhancing features such as upgraded suspension components, sportier styling cues, and possibly a more powerful engine compared to the standard 2000GT model.

  • Nissan Skyline 2000GT-R Prototype: While not a production trim level, the GT-R prototypes built during this time represented the pinnacle of Skyline performance. These prototypes showcased advanced technologies and features intended for a high-performance GT-R model. They may have featured turbocharged engines, upgraded drivetrains, and other performance enhancements.

  • Nissan Skyline Sedan: In addition to the coupe models, there were also sedan variants of the Skyline available during this period. These sedans offered practicality and comfort, often with similar engine options to the coupe models but with a focus on passenger space and cargo capacity.

4. Production:

The second-generation Skyline was introduced in 1963. It was initially produced by the Prince Motor Company before the merger with Nissan in 1966. This generation marked a shift towards more modern styling. The second-gen Skyline adopted a boxier, angular appearance compared to its predecessor. Available body styles included a four-door sedan and a station wagon variant. The design was somewhat similar to the previous model but with a more contemporary touch.

5. Rarity and Collectibility:

The second-generation Nissan Skyline, also known as the Prince Skyline, holds a special place in automotive history. The second-gen Skyline laid the groundwork for the iconic GT-R lineage. The scarcity of GT-R models from this era adds to their collectibility. Enthusiasts appreciate the boxy, angular styling that reflects the automotive trends of the time. The GT-R badge on this generation signifies its high-performance lineage.

8. Racing Heritage:

The Kenmeri Skyline GT-R continued the motorsport legacy of its predecessor, the Hakosuka GT-R (KPGC10), by competing in Japanese touring car racing events during the early 1970s. The racing success of the Kenmeri Skyline GT-R left a lasting legacy that continues to inspire Nissan's high-performance lineup and motorsport endeavors.
