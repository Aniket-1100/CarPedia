Nissan Skyline-2nd Gen

The Nissan Skyline GT-R's second generation, often referred to as the "Kenmeri" generation, covers the period from 1973 to 1977. However, it's important to note that there was no GT-R model produced during this time. The second-generation GT-R was planned, and prototypes were even built, but due to changing economic conditions, tightening emissions regulations, and the impact of the 1973 oil crisis, Nissan decided not to put the GT-R into production. While the second-generation GT-R may not have materialized as planned, its story is an important part of the GT-R's overall legacy, showcasing the challenges and decisions faced by automakers during a tumultuous period in automotive history.

Nissan Skyline-3rd Gen

The Nissan Skyline GT-R R32, produced from 1989 to 1994, is a legendary sports car renowned for its groundbreaking technology and dominant performance. Powered by the iconic RB26DETT engine, a 2.6-liter twin-turbo inline-six, the R32 GT-R produced around 280 horsepower (understated due to the "Gentlemen's Agreement" in Japan) and featured advanced features such as ATTESA E-TS all-wheel drive and Super-HICAS four-wheel steering for exceptional handling.

Nissan Skyline-4th Gen

The Nissan Skyline GT-R R33, produced from 1995 to 1998, is a highly acclaimed sports car known for its refined performance and advanced technology. Powered by the RB26DETT engine, a 2.6-liter twin-turbo inline-six, the R33 GT-R continued the legacy of its predecessor with improvements in power delivery and reliability. The interior offered a driver-focused cockpit with supportive seats and intuitive controls. The R33 GT-R achieved success in motorsport, including victories in the Japanese Touring Car Championship (JTCC), further solidifying its reputation as a capable track performer.

Nissan Skyline-5th Gen

The Nissan Skyline GT-R R34 is a legendary high-performance sports car that was produced by Nissan from 1999 to 2002. It is the successor to the R32 and R33 generations of the Skyline GT-R, and it is considered to be one of the greatest Japanese sports cars of all time. The R34 GT-R was powered by Nissan's legendary RB26DETT engine, a twin-turbocharged 2.6-liter inline-six that produced 280 horsepower (limited due to a Japanese gentlemen's agreement) and 276 lb-ft of torque in stock form.

Nissan Skyline-6th Gen

The Nissan GT-R (Gran Turismo-Racing), also known by its internal code R35, is a high-performance sports car and grand tourer produced by Nissan Motor Company. Unveiled in 2007, it is the sixth iteration of the Nissan Skyline GT-R lineage, but the first to not carry the Skyline name. The GT-R is known for its incredible acceleration, achieving 0-60 mph in as fast as 2.9 seconds. It also has a top speed of over 200 mph, making it one of the fastest production cars on the market.
