Nissan Skyline Third Generation

❮ ❯

This generation marked the rebirth of the GT-R legend. Introduced in 1989, the R32 GT-R was powered by a 2.6-liter twin-turbocharged inline-six engine, known as the RB26DETT, producing around 276 horsepower (though rumored to have significantly more). It featured advanced technology like ATTESA E-TS all-wheel drive and Super-HICAS four-wheel steering, dominating motorsport competitions such as Group A racing. The third generation of the Nissan Skyline, often referred to as the R32 generation, encompasses a wide range of models, including the iconic Skyline GT-R. The GT-R variant of the R32 generation is arguably the most famous and celebrated model. It featured the RB26DETT engine producing around 276 horsepower, advanced all-wheel-drive system, and advanced handling technologies. The R32 GT-R earned the nickname "Godzilla" due to its dominance in motorsport.

1. Engine Options:

The R32 GT-R is powered by Nissan's iconic RB26DETT engine, a 2.6-liter twin-turbocharged inline-six. It produces approximately 276 horsepower and 260 lb-ft of torque, although many enthusiasts believe the actual output to be higher. The engine features dual overhead camshafts and 24 valves. Engine options varied across different trim levels and models. Notable engines included inline-four and inline-six configurations, with displacements ranging from 1.8L to 2.6L.

2. Trim Levels:

The Nissan Skyline GT-R third generation, also known as the R32, was primarily offered in a single trim level, the GT-R. However, within the GT-R lineup, there were a few variations and special editions produced during its production run from 1989 to 1994.
  • Standard R32 GT-R: This is the base model of the R32 GT-R, equipped with the iconic RB26DETT engine, ATTESA E-TS all-wheel-drive system, Super-HICAS four-wheel steering, and other performance-oriented features. It came with standard interior amenities and minimal optional extras.

  • Nismo Edition: Nismo (Nissan Motorsports International Co., Ltd.) offered performance-enhanced versions of the R32 GT-R. These editions typically featured upgraded suspension components, exhaust systems, and aerodynamic enhancements. The Nismo R32 GT-Rs were produced in limited numbers and are highly sought after by enthusiasts.

  • V-Spec (Victory Specification): Introduced in 1993, the V-Spec was a higher-performance variant of the R32 GT-R. It featured upgrades such as larger brakes, stiffer suspension, a front LSD (Limited Slip Differential), and a reinforced body structure. The V-Spec aimed to improve handling and overall performance on the track.

  • V-Spec II: This variant, introduced later in the R32 GT-R's production cycle, further refined the enhancements introduced with the V-Spec. It featured revised suspension settings and additional weight-saving measures, making it even more track-focused than the standard V-Spec.

3. Customization Options:

It features ventilated disc brakes on all four wheels, aided by an advanced ABS (Anti-lock Braking System) for optimal stopping power and control. Interior: The interior of the R32 GT-R is driver-focused, with a functional and ergonomic layout. It includes supportive bucket seats, a sporty steering wheel, and a comprehensive gauge cluster providing vital information to the driver. Exterior: The R32 GT-R sports an iconic design with aggressive styling cues, including a distinctive rear wing, flared wheel arches, and a front bumper with integrated air intakes for improved cooling and aerodynamics.

4. Production:

The production of the Nissan Skyline GT-R R32 began in 1989 and lasted until 1994. During this time, Nissan produced various versions of the R32 GT-R to meet market demands and regulatory requirements. The R32 GT-R was primarily manufactured at Nissan's plant in Tochigi, Japan. This facility was responsible for the assembly of the vehicles, including the installation of the drivetrain, suspension components, interior features, and exterior body panels.

5. Rarity and Collectibility:

The R32 GT-R has attained legendary status among automotive enthusiasts worldwide. Its combination of performance, technology, and iconic design has cemented its place in automotive history and pop culture. The third generation Nissan Skyline, particularly the R32 GT-R, left an indelible mark on automotive history. Its technological innovations, motorsport success, and timeless design have ensured its enduring legacy and continued popularity among enthusiasts worldwide. The R32 GT-R, in particular, remains highly sought after and revered as one of the greatest sports cars of all time.

6. Technology and Performance:

The R32 Skyline introduced advanced technology for its time, such as ATTESA E-TS (Advanced Total Traction Engineering System for All-Terrain with Electronic Torque Split) all-wheel-drive system and HICAS (High Capacity Actively Controlled Steering) four-wheel steering. Performance: The Skyline GT-R R32 became particularly famous for its outstanding performance on both the road and the track. With its RB26DETT engine, advanced drivetrain, and chassis dynamics, it quickly gained a reputation as a "supercar killer."

7. Global Recognition:

The R32 GT-R's racing success garnered international recognition and admiration. Its dominance on the track earned it the nickname "Godzilla," a testament to its imposing presence and performance prowess. Enthusiasts and motorsport fans from various regions of the world were captivated by the R32 GT-R's achievements, cementing its status as a legendary racing car. The racing heritage of the R32 GT-R continues to influence automotive culture and performance car enthusiasts worldwide. Its success on the track inspired future generations of GT-R models and elevated the status of the GT-R brand.

8. Racing Heritage:

The R32 GT-R achieved unprecedented success in Group A racing, particularly in the Japanese Touring Car Championship (JTCC) and the Australian Touring Car Championship (ATCC). In the JTCC, the R32 GT-R won all 29 races it entered from 1990 to 1993, showcasing its unrivaled performance and reliability. In the ATCC, the R32 GT-R made a significant impact, winning the championship in 1991 and 1992 against formidable competition.

9. Transmission:

The transmission in the Nissan Skyline GT-R R32 plays a crucial role in delivering power from the engine to the wheels efficiently. The R32 GT-R is equipped with a 5-speed manual transmission, which allows drivers to manually select gears for optimal performance and control. Manual transmissions were preferred in high-performance sports cars like the GT-R for their direct engagement and involvement in the driving experience.

10. Distinctive Design:

The Nissan Skyline GT-R R32 is renowned for its distinctive design, which combines aerodynamic efficiency with aggressive styling cues. The R32 GT-R features a bold and aggressive front end characterized by its signature quad-round headlights, which became a hallmark of the GT-R lineage. The front bumper incorporates large air intakes and a prominent grille, not only providing ample airflow to the engine but also enhancing the car's menacing appearance. To accommodate wider tires and improve handling, the R32 GT-R boasts flared wheel arches that give the car a muscular and athletic stance. These flared wheel arches are not only functional but also contribute to the GT-R's aggressive and purposeful look.
